Entrée Box Design Guide

Your Entrée Boxes are the stars of the show.
Here's how to design them for the spotlight.

There are four acclaimed eateries in every ForestParkPicnics.com basket. Your box is one of two entrées in a 3-course meal for two. The other courses are already handled — your job is to wow them, not fill them up.

1

Choose quality over quantity.

This Entrée Box is one of two shared in the 2nd course of a 3-course meal. There's already a shared appetizer course and a pint of ice cream for dessert. You don't have to fill anyone up.

Think 3–4 bites per person = 6–8 bites per box for each entrée and side. Often, this is a ½ sandwich split in ½ again and wrapped or containerized individually.

2

Design for temperature loss.

There will be 30 minutes between the time we pick up the boxes and the time picnickers receive them — imagine another 10–30 minutes before they're eating their entrées. While we manage temperature at every step (including thermal bags in the basket), there are no microwaves or air fryers on Art Hill.

We are specifically looking for entrées almost as good warm as they are when hot.

We sample every entrée after one hour inside the box + inside a thermal bag before selecting it.

3

Keep the box clean.

No bags, napkins, cutlery, or condiments that weren't made in-house specifically for this entrée are to be included. We handle everything else in the basket — guests arrive with everything they need.

4

We handle the box logistics.

We will drop off a red thermal bag with the proper number of sanitized & lined stainless steel boxes a minimum of one hour before (10am / 4pm) your scheduled pickup. The front window of the bag will display:

  • The order details
  • Your specific portioning diagram
  • Pickup time
  • A check attached for the total amount due
